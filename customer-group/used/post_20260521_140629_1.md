An old Cherokee tale tells of a grandfather teaching his grandson about life.

'A fight is going on inside me,' the grandfather says. 'It is a terrible fight between two wolves. One is evil: anger, envy, sorrow, regret, greed, arrogance, self-pity, guilt, resentment, inferiority, lies, false pride, superiority, and ego. The other is good: joy, peace, love, hope, serenity, humility, kindness, benevolence, empathy, generosity, truth, compassion, and faith.'

The grandson thinks for a moment, then asks: 'Which wolf wins?'

The grandfather replies: 'The one you feed.'

Trading reveals which wolf you've been feeding.

When a trade goes against you, which wolf speaks up? The one that says you're stupid, you should have known better, you'll never make it? Or the one that says this is data, this is learning, this is part of the process?

When you see someone else winning, which wolf gets fed? The one that envies, that compares, that resents? Or the one that celebrates, that learns, that knows their time is coming?

The beautiful and terrifying thing is: you choose. Every moment of every day. Every trade. Every reaction.

Which wolf have you been feeding lately?
