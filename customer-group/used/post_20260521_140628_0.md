There's a story about Chinese bamboo farmers that every trader should know.

For five years, the farmer waters his bamboo seeds every single day. Every. Single. Day. And for five years, nothing happens. Not a sprout. Not a sign of life. Just dirt.

Most people would have quit by year two. Maybe year three if they're stubborn. But the farmer keeps watering.

Then, in year five, something remarkable happens. The bamboo shoots up 90 feet in just six weeks.

The question is: did it grow 90 feet in six weeks? Or did it grow 90 feet in five years?

Trading works the same way. The months you're studying, backtesting, journaling, losing small, learning, adjusting... it feels like nothing is happening. Like you're watering dirt.

But beneath the surface, roots are forming. A foundation is being built. And when that breakthrough finally comes, it can feel sudden. But it wasn't. It was the result of every single day you showed up.

If you're in the watering phase right now, keep going. The growth is coming. You just can't see it yet.
