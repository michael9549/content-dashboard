The market is a mirror. It reflects back exactly who you are, not who you pretend to be.

In your day job, you can fake it. Smile when you're frustrated. Nod when you're confused. Say 'I'm fine' when you're falling apart. People believe the mask because they want to. It's easier that way.

But the market doesn't care about your mask. It doesn't care about your intentions, your potential, or your promises to yourself. It only responds to your actions.

When you're greedy, the market shows you. When you're fearful, the market shows you. When you're disciplined, when you're patient, when you're impulsive, when you're arrogant... the market shows you. Every single time.

This is why trading takes time to master. Not because the charts are complicated. Not because the strategies are complex. But because the only way to improve is to look honestly at the reflection.

And most people spend their whole lives avoiding honest mirrors.

The traders who succeed aren't the ones who found the perfect indicator. They're the ones who developed the courage to look at their reflection without flinching. To see their greed, their fear, their impatience, their ego... and to work on those things directly.

Because when you change who you are, your trading changes automatically.
