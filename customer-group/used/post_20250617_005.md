Your trading journal is worth more than any indicator.

Every trade you take teaches you something. But only if you write it down. The trader who journals learns from every mistake. The trader who doesn't repeats the same mistakes for years.

Here's what happens without a journal:

You take a trade. It loses. You feel bad. You move on. A week later, you take a similar trade. It loses again. You still don't see the pattern. Six months later, you've made the same mistake twenty times and wonder why your account isn't growing.

Here's what happens with a journal:

You take a trade. You log the setup, your emotions, your risk, the outcome. You review weekly. You spot the pattern: you keep entering too early before confirmation. You adjust. Your results improve.

The difference is data. Objective, written data that doesn't lie to you.

Your memory is not reliable. It rewrites history to protect your ego. Your journal doesn't. It shows you exactly what you did, when you did it, and why.

Every professional trader keeps detailed records. Not because they enjoy paperwork, but because they know: you can't improve what you don't measure. And you can't measure what you don't record.

Your journal should include: setup type, entry reason, risk amount, emotions before/during/after, and lessons learned. Review it weekly. The patterns will shock you.

Do you keep a trading journal? If not, what's stopping you from starting today?
