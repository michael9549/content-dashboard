If you only celebrate the huge wins, trading will always feel discouraging.

Big moves are fun, but they do not build your account on their own.

What actually builds your equity curve are the **boring, controlled, rule based wins** you stack over and over.

Things like:

- Following your stop loss without moving it
- Taking partials exactly where your plan said to
- Skipping a bad setup even though it "looked tempting"

Every time you do that, you are training yourself to be the kind of person who can handle larger size later.

Here is a simple exercise for this week:

Each day, write down **one small win** related to your behavior, not just your PnL.

For example:

- "Waited for confirmation on Sniper Pro, Quantum, and ATH instead of jumping in early"
- "Ignored three alerts that did not fit my plan"

By the end of the week, you will have proof that you are becoming a more disciplined trader, even if the dollar amount has not caught up yet.

Comment "WINS" if you are willing to start tracking the small victories that actually move you forward.
