The One Thing That Finally Made Me Profitable

I traded for many years before I became consistently profitable.

Many years of blown accounts. Many years of strategy-hopping. Many years of thinking I just needed to find the "right" system.

Then one day, something clicked.

It wasn't a new indicator. It wasn't a secret pattern. 

**It was position sizing.**

Specifically: Risking the same small percentage on every single trade, no matter how "sure" I was.

Before this, I would size up when I was "confident" and size down when I was unsure. Which meant my biggest losses came on my biggest positions. Classic.

Once I started risking 1% per trade, everything changed.

I could take 5 losses in a row and barely feel it. I could let setups come to me instead of forcing trades. I could actually think clearly during trades because I wasn't terrified of the loss.

**The math is simple:** If you risk 0.5% per trade, you can be wrong 20 times in a row and only be down 10%. You'll still have 90% of your account to trade with.

If you risk 5% per trade and hit a 5-loss streak (which happens), you're down 25%. Now you're emotional. Now you're trying to "make it back." Now you're done.

**Your position size determines your psychology more than your psychology determines your position size.**

If you're struggling with emotions during trades, check your risk per trade first. I bet it's too high.
