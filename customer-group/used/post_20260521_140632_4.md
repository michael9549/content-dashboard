Every skill follows a predictable pattern. At first, progress feels rapid. You're learning so much every day. The gains are visible, exciting, motivating.

Then, something happens. The rapid progress stops. You enter what researchers call 'the plateau.'

Days go by. Weeks. Maybe months. You're putting in the same effort. Maybe even more. But the results don't come. It feels like you've hit a ceiling. Like maybe this is as good as you'll ever get.

This is the moment that separates those who master a skill from those who quit.

Because here's what most people don't understand: the plateau isn't empty time. It's not wasted effort. The plateau is where the real work happens. It's where your brain is reorganizing, deepening, integrating. It's where competence transforms into mastery.

Think of it like heating water. From 0 to 99 degrees, nothing much seems to happen. The water gets warmer, sure, but it looks the same. Then at 100 degrees, it transforms into steam.

The plateau is your 99 degrees. It feels like nothing is changing. But you're one degree away from transformation.

In trading, the plateau might look like: you've learned the strategy, you understand the concepts, but your results are inconsistent. You're not losing big, but you're not winning big either. You're stuck.

This is normal. This is expected. This is part of the process.

The question isn't whether you'll hit plateaus. You will. The question is: will you keep heating the water?

What would it look like to trust the plateau you're in right now?
