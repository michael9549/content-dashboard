There's a psychological phenomenon called 'the weight of expectation.'

When you place too much importance on a single event, a single trade, a single day... it becomes heavy. Too heavy to handle properly. Your hands shake. Your mind races. You make decisions based on fear of loss rather than probability.

The trader who needs tomorrow's trade to pay rent is not the same trader who trades with a six-month cushion. Same knowledge. Same charts. Same setup. Completely different execution.

This is why risk management isn't just about protecting capital. It's about protecting your psychology. It's about keeping each trade light enough that you can execute without the weight crushing you.

When you risk too much on a single trade, you're not just risking money. You're risking your clarity. Your objectivity. Your ability to make good decisions.

The market can sense desperation. It feeds on it.

Professional traders understand this. They size their positions so that no single outcome matters that much. A win is nice. A loss is data. Neither one changes their life. Neither one changes who they are.

This lightness is a competitive advantage. When you're not desperate, you can see clearly. When you're not fearful, you can act decisively. When you're not attached to the outcome, you can follow your system.

What weight are you carrying into your trades that you need to set down?
