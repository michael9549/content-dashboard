# Your Expectations Are Destroying Your Progress

"I want to make $1,000 this week."

"I should be profitable by now."

"Why is everyone else winning and I'm not?"

These expectations are poison.

---

**Trading doesn't care about your timeline.**

The market isn't obligated to give you profits because you need to pay rent. It doesn't care that you "should" be further along by now.

Your job isn't to force results. It's to follow your process and let results come when they come.

---

**Unrealistic expectations lead to:**

Overtrading (trying to force opportunities)
Oversizing (risking too much to hit arbitrary goals)
Emotional decisions (trading from desperation, not strategy)
Quitting (when reality doesn't match the fantasy)

---

**The fix:**

Focus on process goals, not outcome goals.

Instead of "I need to make $500 this week," try "I will take only A+ setups this week."

Instead of "I should be profitable by month 3," try "I will review every trade honestly and learn from each one."

Process goals you control. Outcome goals? Not so much.

---

**Trade the market you have, not the market you want.**

Some weeks there are setups. Some weeks there aren't. Your expectations won't change that.

---

**Drop a 🎯 if you're shifting from outcome goals to process goals.**

Focus on what you control. Let go of what you don't.
