# FOMO Is Costing You More Than Bad Trades

Let me guess.

You missed a move.
Maybe you were asleep. Maybe you were at work. Maybe you just weren't paying attention.

And now you're watching it run without you.

Your brain starts spinning:
"I should've been in that."
"I knew that was going to happen."
"I can't believe I missed it."

So you do what feels logical in the moment:
You chase it.

You enter late. You widen your stop. You size up to "make up for it."

And that's when the market teaches you an expensive lesson.

---

**Here's what the pros know:**

There will ALWAYS be another setup.
Always.

Missing one trade doesn't matter.
Taking a bad trade because you missed the last one? That matters a lot.

The market isn't going anywhere.
Your account, on the other hand, can disappear fast.

---

**The reframe:**

Instead of "I missed out," try "that wasn't my setup."

Your edge isn't catching every move.
Your edge is catching the moves that fit YOUR criteria.

Everything else is just noise.
