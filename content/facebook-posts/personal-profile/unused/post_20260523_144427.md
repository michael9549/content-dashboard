Here is a hard truth about AI that most people miss.

Liam Ottley, who runs Morningside AI and has implemented AI for some of the world's biggest brands, says the difference between the 5% of companies seeing massive ROI on AI and the 95% who are failing is simple.

They start with process, not technology.

Most businesses get this completely backwards. They get hyped about a new tool, dive straight into development, and fail. The initial shine of AI is gone now. This stuff takes real human skill to get right. Even OpenAI admitted this by launching their own consulting team.

Ottley breaks down a three-step framework for automating any business with AI.

Step one is audit and map. Before you touch any AI tool, you document your current processes. What are the repetitive tasks? Where are the bottlenecks? What decisions get made over and over? Most companies skip this and wonder why their AI implementation flops.

Step two is identify automation opportunities. Not everything should be automated. The best candidates are high-volume, rule-based tasks that drain human energy. Customer support triage. Data entry. Content repurposing. Lead qualification. The stuff that makes your team want to quit.

Step three is build and iterate. Start small. One workflow. One process. Prove it works. Then expand. The companies that try to automate everything at once are the ones that end up with expensive, broken systems that nobody uses.

The 5% who win treat AI like any other business tool. They define the problem first, then find the technology that solves it. The 95% who fail buy the technology first, then go looking for problems to justify the purchase.

If you are a business owner, the question is not "What AI tool should I use?" The question is "What repetitive, draining process in my business could be handled by a system instead of a person?"

If you are an entrepreneur, the opportunity is massive. The 95% who are failing need help. They bought the tools. They do not know how to use them. That gap is where the money is.
