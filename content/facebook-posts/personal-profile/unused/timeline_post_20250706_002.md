# Why 95% of AI Initiatives Fail (And How to Be in the 5%)

This breakdown from Liam at Morningside AI cuts through the hype. Everyone is talking about AI automation, but almost nobody is getting results. The numbers are brutal: MIT research shows 95% of AI initiatives in businesses fail to deliver any return on investment.

That is not because the technology is overhyped. It is because companies are doing it backwards.

Here is the pattern. A business gets excited about a new AI tool. They skip the strategy, skip the planning, and dive straight into building. They hire developers, buy subscriptions, and start automating things that do not matter. Six months later, they have spent money, disrupted their team, and have nothing to show for it. Then they declare that AI does not work for their industry.

The 5% who are winning take a completely different approach. They start with process, not technology. Before writing a single line of code or connecting any API, they map out how their business actually works. They interview employees, document workflows, and identify where the real bottlenecks are. Only then do they look for AI solutions that fit those specific problems.

This sounds obvious, but almost nobody does it. Most businesses are organized chaos. Data lives in twelve different apps that do not talk to each other. Processes are a mix of how we have always done it and whatever tool someone signed up for last month. Employees are secretly using AI tools on their own, creating data leak risks and inconsistent outputs. Trying to plug AI into that mess is like installing a Ferrari engine in a car with no wheels.

The Morningside method is a three-phase playbook. Phase one is education. Get the leadership team aligned on what AI actually is, what it can do, and what the strategic vision looks like. Not the hype. The reality. Phase two is identification. Map the business processes, find the bottlenecks, and grade opportunities by impact and difficulty. Phase three is development. Start with quick wins, not moonshots. Build something small that delivers ROI fast, then use that momentum for bigger projects.

The quick wins are almost never the sexy projects. They are not building an AI agent that replaces your sales team. They are automating the soul-crushing manual data entry that eats up thirty hours a week. They are creating a voice agent that handles the let me look that up for you calls. They are building a document query system so employees stop wasting time searching through folders. Boring problems, massive impact.

For entrepreneurs watching this, there is a massive opportunity. Millions of businesses are struggling with AI implementation. They want the results but have no idea how to get there. If you can learn this process and help them execute it, you are not just selling a service. You are becoming a strategic partner.

The question is not whether AI will transform your business. It is whether you will be in the 5% who do it right, or the 95% who waste time and money chasing shiny tools.

What is one boring, repetitive task in your business that AI could handle today?
