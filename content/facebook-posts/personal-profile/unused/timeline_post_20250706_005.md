# The One Sales Skill That Separates the Best From the Rest

This video breaks down a sales technique that the creator says he has never taught before. It is called reframing, and it is the difference between a salesperson who gets rejected constantly and one who closes at a high rate.

The core idea is simple. When a prospect says anything other than yes, you do not answer their question directly. Instead, you reframe the conversation with one to three sentences that increase the likelihood your next statement gets them to buy.

Here is the three A framework.

First, acknowledge. Repeat back what they said. This does two things. It shows you are listening, and it buys you time to think. If someone says they need to think about it, you say I totally understand, you want to make sure this is the right decision. That is the acknowledge step.

Second, associate. Connect their question or objection to the behavior of your best customers. This is the most powerful part. When they feel like they are stepping away from buying, you say that is actually a question a lot of our best customers ask. It means you are thinking about this the right way. Now they are not an outsider pushing back. They are someone who resembles your most successful buyers.

Third, ask. After you have acknowledged and associated, you ask a question about their question. If they ask about certifications, you ask which certifications are you looking for specifically? If they say timing is bad, you ask what would make this a good time? If they need to talk to their spouse, you ask what parts do you think they would disagree with?

The reason this works is control. The person asking the questions is the one controlling the conversation. Most salespeople go on defense the moment a prospect pushes back. They start answering questions they do not know the answer to, and they let the prospect become the judge of whether those answers are good enough. That is a losing position.

The video also covers five rules for using this ethically.

Rule one, the prospect believes almost nothing you say and almost everything they say. Your job is not to tell them they are a good fit. Your job is to ask questions until they say I think I am a good fit for this.

Rule two, no one can disagree with a question. If you ever feel stuck, ask more questions. You can never disagree with a question. This keeps you from arguing with the prospect, which you will always lose.

Rule three, tell them what their question means. When they say I need to think about it, you reframe it as I understand, you want to make sure you are making the right decision. That is smart. What is your main concern? Now you are back in the sale.

Rule four, use straw men for tough truths. If you need to challenge their thinking, do not challenge them directly. Talk about another customer who had the same concern. It creates a neutral third party they will not get defensive about.

Rule five, never sell unqualified prospects. This only works if you are telling the truth. If someone is not a good fit, this technique should help you both discover that quickly. It is not about manipulation. It is about helping people make good decisions.

The video closes with a powerful point. The best closers make the most asks. But they do it in a way that maintains rapport, so they can keep asking without the prospect hating them.
