I used to think marketing changes fast, but I have never seen anything like 2025.

Wes McDowell says strategies that were working perfectly six months ago have completely fallen apart. And the businesses thriving right now are not the ones with the biggest budgets or the most followers. They are the ones who adapted fastest.

McDowell breaks down five brand new digital marketing strategies that are working right now.

Strategy one: AI-powered personalization at scale. Not just putting someone's first name in an email. Real personalization where AI analyzes behavior, predicts needs, and delivers the right message at the right time. The companies doing this are seeing open rates and conversion rates that would have been impossible two years ago.

Strategy two: short-form video as the primary discovery channel. McDowell says the funnel has flipped. People used to find you through Google, then follow you on social media. Now they find you on TikTok, Instagram Reels, or YouTube Shorts, then go to Google to verify. If you are not creating short-form video, you are basically invisible to the next generation of buyers.

Strategy three: community over audience. Building a following is old news. Building a community is what matters now. McDowell says the most successful brands in 2025 are treating their customers like members, not buyers. Private groups, exclusive content, direct access. The feeling of belonging is the new competitive advantage.

Strategy four: value-first monetization. Instead of gatekeeping all your best content behind a paywall, give away your best stuff for free and monetize the implementation. Teach the what for free. Sell the how. The creators winning right now are the most generous ones, not the most guarded ones.

Strategy five: AI-assisted content creation with human oversight. McDowell is clear about this. AI should do 80% of the work, but the final 20% needs a human touch. The brands that are publishing pure AI content without editing are starting to see their engagement drop. People can smell it. The brands that use AI to speed up production but add human perspective and personality are winning.

The common thread: what worked in 2024 is not working in 2025. The platforms have changed. The algorithms have changed. Consumer behavior has changed. The only constant is that the people who adapt fastest win.
