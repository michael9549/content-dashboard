Myron Golden made millions. Not because he got lucky. Because he changed how he thinks.

He breaks down seven mindset shifts that transformed his life. And the first one is the hardest for most people to accept.

Mindset shift one: your past does not determine your future. Golden says most people are living in the prison of their past experiences. They failed once, so they believe they will fail again. Someone told them they were not good enough, so they never tried. Your past is data, not destiny. The only thing it determines is what you have already done, not what you are capable of doing next.

Mindset shift two: poverty is a mindset, not a circumstance. Golden grew up poor. He says the difference between people who stay poor and people who break out is not opportunity. It is belief. Poor people believe money is scarce. Wealthy people believe money is abundant and flows to those who solve problems. Same world. Different operating system.

Mindset shift three: you are not paid for your time. You are paid for the value you create. This one stings because most people have been conditioned to think in terms of hourly wages. Golden says if you want to make more, stop thinking about how many hours you work and start thinking about how much value you deliver in those hours. One hour of high-value work beats 10 hours of low-value work every time.

Mindset shift four: selling is serving. Most people feel guilty about selling. Golden says that is backwards. If your product or service actually helps people, not selling it is selfish. You are withholding something that could improve someone's life because you are afraid of feeling pushy.

Mindset shift five: invest in yourself first. Before you buy the car, the watch, the vacation, invest in skills, knowledge, and relationships that increase your earning capacity. Golden says most people have this backwards. They buy liabilities first and wonder why they never have money for assets.

The common thread through all seven shifts is this: wealth is built in the mind before it is built in the bank. Change your thinking, and your results follow. Try to change your results without changing your thinking, and you will keep getting what you have always gotten.
