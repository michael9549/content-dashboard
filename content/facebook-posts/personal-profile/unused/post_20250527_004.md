Alex Hormozi just shared the sales tactic that took him from good to 100% close rate.

And he learned it by accident.

This was back when he was selling supplements at his gym. He had a nutrition orientation process where hed set up meal plans and recommend products. He was already selling well, but he wanted more.

Then something happened that changed everything.

He ran out of one of his key products right before a big launch. A hundred new customers were coming in, and he didn't have enough inventory.

Here's what he did instead of panic: he told the truth.

He explained to customers that he was out of stock, but he wanted to be honest about what he would have recommended and why. He walked them through exactly what the product did, how it helped, and why he believed in it.

Then he told them where they could buy it elsewhere.

That's it. No pressure. No pitch. Just genuine education and honesty.

And his close rate jumped to 100%.

He taught this same approach to people who had never sold before, and they started closing 80-90% of prospects. In a retail environment. With physical products.

The tactic? Radical transparency.

Most salespeople are trying to extract value. They're focused on the transaction, the commission, the close. Prospects can smell it from a mile away.

But when you shift to educating, to truly helping, to being willing to send someone elsewhere if that's what's best for them... something changes.

Trust happens instantly.

Hormozi calls this the "$100 million sales tactic" because it's the foundation of everything he's built. And it started with running out of inventory and having no choice but to be honest.

Sometimes the best strategies come from constraints, not abundance.
