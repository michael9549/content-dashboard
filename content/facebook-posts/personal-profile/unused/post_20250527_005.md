Natalie Dawson went from making $55,000 a year to millions through mastering sales.

And she started out terrified of rejection.

She just broke down 9 strategies that changed everything for her. But the first one hit me so hard I had to share it immediately.

Flip the script on what sales actually is.

Most people think sales is gross. Manipulative. Pushy. And they're right... if you don't believe in what you're selling.

Think about being a car salesperson selling cars to people who can't afford them. Of course that feels gross. You're creating problems, not solving them.

But now imagine you're selling Lamborghinis at a premium dealership. You believe in the product. You know your buyers can afford it. You're not pushing anyone into anything they don't want.

The sale becomes fun. Impactful. You're solving a problem for someone who genuinely wants the solution.

Here's the quote that changed everything for Natalie (from Grant Cardone):

"To the extent that you are sold, you will sell."

Read that again.

If your sales numbers are bad, it's not because you're a bad salesperson. It's because you're not sold on your own product.

When you truly believe that what you're offering helps people, everything changes. You're not convincing anyone. You're helping them make a decision that gets them closer to the life they want.

You become the conduit for their transformation.

So the question isn't "how do I get better at sales techniques?"

The question is: do you actually believe in what you're selling?

If the answer is no, either find a way to get sold on it, or sell something else. Life's too short to spend your days pushing products you don't believe in.

When you're bought in, sales stops being about you and starts being about them. And that's when the magic happens.

How sold are you on what you're offering right now? Be honest.
