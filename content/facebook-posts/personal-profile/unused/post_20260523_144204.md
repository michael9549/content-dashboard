Most people think selling is about having the best product or the lowest price.

Adam Erhart breaks down why that is completely wrong in his breakdown of dark psychology sales techniques. And before you worry, this is not about manipulation. It is about understanding how people actually make decisions.

The part that stuck with me: people do not buy when they see a bright future. They buy when staying where they are feels more painful than changing.

Think about that for a second.

How many people do you know who are "fine" with their current situation? They make okay money. Their life is not falling apart. But underneath, they hate Mondays. They feel invisible at work. They scroll mindlessly at night to distract themselves from the fact that this does not feel like their life anymore.

If you ask them how things are going, they say "Yeah, I am doing all right."

But if you ask them what happens if they are still doing the exact same thing in three years?

Silence.

That is the crack. That is the moment when "all right" stops feeling all right at all.

Erhart calls this moving someone from "latent pain" to "realized pain." The pain was always there. They just did not see it yet. And once they feel it emotionally, not just understand it intellectually, they start searching for a way out.

Another insight that hit hard: people do not want to be sold to. They want to feel like they are the ones making the decision.

So instead of pushing your offer, you guide the conversation while making them feel in control. Ask permission before sharing advice. Give them choices where all roads lead back to you. Position your offer as a path, not a demand.

The video also talks about something called "future pacing." Instead of saying "I will help you grow your business," you paint the scene: "Imagine waking up and your funnel is already making sales while you are out walking your dog. No more scrambling. No more guessing."

People do not buy coaching or courses or services. They buy a future version of themselves. Your job is to make that future feel real enough to reach for.

One more thing that stood out. The "adventurer frame" instead of the "expert frame." You do not need to be the guru at the top of the mountain. You can be the person one step ahead, holding the flashlight, documenting the journey in real time. Sometimes that is more relatable. Sometimes that is exactly what people need.

The whole video is worth watching if you sell anything. Not because you need to use dark tactics, but because you need to understand the psychology behind why people say yes.
