Jim Rohn said success is not something you chase. It is something you attract by the person you become.

That sounds like a motivational poster, but there is real substance behind it. Rohn spent decades studying what separates people who build wealth from people who stay stuck. And the difference is not luck, talent, or connections. It is mindset.

Rohn breaks down three methods to develop what he calls the millionaire mindset.

Method one: expand your vision. Most people think too small because they are surrounded by small thinkers. Rohn says if you want to change your results, change your references. Read books by people who have done what you want to do. Listen to podcasts by people playing at a higher level. Spend time with people who think bigger than you do. Your vision is the ceiling of your potential. Most people have a vision that is way too low.

Method two: break through mental barriers. Rohn says every person has invisible walls in their mind. "I am not good with money." "I am not a salesperson." "I do not have what it takes." These are not facts. They are stories. And the problem is that most people treat their stories as permanent truths instead of temporary beliefs. The millionaire mindset requires you to question your own limitations. Where did that belief come from? Is it actually true? What would be possible if it were not?

Method three: develop discipline. Rohn is famous for saying "Discipline is the bridge between goals and accomplishment." Motivation gets you started. Discipline keeps you going when motivation fades. The millionaire mindset is not about feeling inspired every day. It is about doing the work even when you do not feel like it. Especially when you do not feel like it.

Rohn has a way of making complex ideas feel simple. Not because they are easy, but because they are clear. Expand your vision. Break your barriers. Build your discipline. Do those three things consistently, and the results follow.

The question is not whether the formula works. The question is whether you will work the formula.
