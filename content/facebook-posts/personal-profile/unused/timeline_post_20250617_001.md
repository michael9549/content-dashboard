Came across this conversation about money that hit different.

Scott Galloway made a distinction that stopped me in my tracks: rich is what you see. Wealth is what you don't.

Rich is the car, the house, the watch. Wealth is passive income that exceeds your burn. One is a performance. The other is security.

We keep chasing the visible symbols because they're easy to measure. You can point to a house. You can't point to economic security. But which one actually lets you sleep at night?

Galloway's definition of wealth: passive income greater than your expenses. That's it. Not a number. Not a milestone. Just income that covers your life without you trading time for it.

The pressure to own a home, to check that box, to follow the script. It's situational. What works in one city at one income level might be a suicide pact in another. The symbol became the goal, and we stopped asking if the goal actually fit our life.

What's fascinating is how this applies beyond money. We pursue visible achievements in every area. The title, the follower count, the revenue number. But the real metric is always the one you can't post about.

What's something you pursued because it looked like success, only to realize it wasn't your version of it?
