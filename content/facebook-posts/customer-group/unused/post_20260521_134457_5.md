Three years ago, I almost gave up on trading forever.

I had been at it for 18 months. Studied every course I could find. Watched hundreds of hours of videos. Backtested strategies until my eyes hurt. And I was still losing money. Not a lot. But consistently. Month after month. My account was bleeding slowly, and so was my confidence.

I remember sitting on my couch one night, laptop closed, wondering if I should just accept that this wasn't for me. Maybe I wasn't smart enough. Maybe I didn't have the right personality. Maybe I should just stick to my 9-to-5 and forget this whole dream of financial freedom.

But something stopped me from quitting. A stubborn voice that said, 'Not yet. One more try.' So I made a deal with myself. I'd give it six more months. But this time, I'd do it differently. No more jumping between strategies. No more chasing the next shiny object. Just one simple system, executed with discipline, every single day.

Those six months changed my life. Not because I suddenly became a genius trader. But because I finally committed. I stopped looking for the easy way and embraced the process. If you're on the edge of giving up, maybe you just haven't truly committed yet. What's one thing you could commit to for the next six months?
