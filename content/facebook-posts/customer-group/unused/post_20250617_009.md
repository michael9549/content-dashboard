The trader who chases the market is always one step behind.

By the time you see a big move on your screen, it's already happened. The opportunity is gone. The risk has shifted. What looks like a trend is often the tail end of a move that's about to reverse.

This is why chasing feels exciting but costs money. You're entering when everyone else is exiting. You're buying when smart money is selling. You're reacting to what already happened instead of positioning for what might happen next.

The alternative is preparation. You identify your levels before the market opens. You know where you want to enter, where you'll exit if wrong, and where you'll take profits if right. When price reaches your zone, you act. If it doesn't, you wait.

This is boring. It doesn't give you the dopamine hit of catching a moving train. But it keeps your money safe and your mind clear.

Think about a lion hunting. It doesn't chase every gazelle it sees. It waits in the tall grass, invisible and patient. It lets the prey come close. Then it strikes with precision. One burst of energy, perfectly timed.

The gazelle that already ran past is not the lion's target. It's focused on the one that's approaching.

Your trading levels are your tall grass. Your patience is your camouflage. The market will come to your zone if you wait.

How often do you find yourself entering trades because price is "already moving" rather than because it reached your predetermined level?
