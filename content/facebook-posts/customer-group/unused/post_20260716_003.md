The Lake and the River

A river that moves fast is exciting. You can hear it from a distance. It cuts through rock, reshapes valleys, carries entire trees downstream. A lake, by comparison, seems passive. It just sits there. But over time, the lake outlasts every flood.

Most traders want to be rivers. They want speed. They want drama. They want the account that doubles in a month because that proves they are good. What they do not want is the lake: slow, patient, almost invisible in its growth.

Risk management is the choice to be a lake.

It is not exciting to risk one percent per trade. It does not feel like freedom to look at a setup and walk away because the stop loss would need to be too wide. It does not feel powerful to close a trade at your predetermined target when the chart is still screaming higher. But the lake does not care about excitement. The lake cares about depth.

Every blown account starts with a single decision to break a rule about risk. Maybe it was adding size because the setup looked perfect. Maybe it was holding through a stop because you were sure it would turn around. Maybe it was trading without a stop at all because this time felt different. It always feels different right before it is not.

The math of risk is simple but cruel. A fifty percent drawdown requires a one hundred percent return just to get back to even. The river that floods its banks does not get to keep the extra water. It just destroys the foundation.

Your position size is not a reflection of your confidence. It is a reflection of your humility.

Are you building a lake, or are you hoping to be the river that does not flood?
