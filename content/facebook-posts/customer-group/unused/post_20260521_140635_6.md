Imagine you're lost in the woods with a compass. You know your destination is north. So you start walking.

But the woods are thick. There are obstacles. Fallen trees. Dense brush. Steep ravines. So you have to go east for a while to get around something. Then west. Sometimes you backtrack because you hit a dead end.

If someone were watching your path from above, it would look like you're going nowhere. Like you're wandering aimlessly. Like you have no idea what you're doing.

But you have the compass. You know where north is. Every step, even the sideways ones, even the backward ones, is taking you closer to your destination.

Trading is the same.

Your compass is your system. Your edge. Your understanding of what works.

But the market doesn't move in straight lines. Some weeks you'll be up. Some weeks you'll be flat. Some weeks you'll give back gains. From the outside, it looks like randomness. Like luck. Like you don't know what you're doing.

But if you trust your compass, if you know your direction is right, then the sideways moves are just part of the path. The drawdowns are just ravines you have to navigate around. The losing streaks are just dense brush that will eventually clear.

The traders who fail are the ones who throw away their compass at the first obstacle. They switch directions. They chase new paths. They never give themselves the chance to break through the woods.

Do you trust your compass? Or are you still looking for a clearer path?
