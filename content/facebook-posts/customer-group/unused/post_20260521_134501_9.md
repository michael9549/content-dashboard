I want to talk about the real reason most traders fail.

It's not the strategy. It's not the market conditions. It's not even the risk management, though that's important. The real reason is simpler and harder to fix: they don't truly believe they can do it. Deep down, there's a voice that says, 'This works for others, but not for me.' And that voice becomes a self-fulfilling prophecy.

I know because I had that voice. Loud and clear. Every time I took a loss, it got louder. 'See? You knew this would happen.' Every time I saw someone else winning, it whispered, 'They have something you don't.' That voice was my constant companion for the first two years of my trading journey.

The breakthrough came when I stopped trying to silence the voice and started challenging it. Every time it spoke up, I'd ask: 'Is that true? Or is that just a story I'm telling myself?' Slowly, I started collecting evidence that contradicted the voice. Small wins. Progress in my consistency. Proof that I COULD do this.

Over time, the voice got quieter. It never fully went away. But it stopped controlling me. And my trading transformed. So what story are you telling yourself? And more importantly: is it actually true? Or is it just keeping you small?
