There's a difference between trying to trade and being a trader.

When you're trying to trade, every setback is evidence that you should quit. Every loss confirms your suspicion that you're not cut out for this. Every bad day is a sign to look for something else.

When you're a trader, setbacks are part of the job. Losses are tuition. Bad days are just Tuesdays. You don't question whether you should keep going because going is who you are.

This isn't just semantics. It's not a trick of language. Your identity shapes your behavior more than your goals do.

Goals are things you want to achieve. Identities are who you believe you are. And we act in alignment with who we believe we are, not what we want to have.

The person who wants to write a book writes when they feel inspired. The writer writes every day because that's what writers do.

The person who wants to get fit goes to the gym when it's convenient. The athlete trains because that's what athletes do.

The person who wants to trade profitably takes trades when the setup looks good. The trader follows their system because that's what traders do.

The question isn't: what results do you want?

The question is: who do you need to become to get those results?

And more importantly: what would that person do today?

If you were already the trader you want to be, how would you handle your next trade?
