Results are noisy. Process is reliable.

In trading, it is easy to become obsessed with outcomes. Did I win or lose? How much did I make? Am I up or down for the month? These questions feel important because they are concrete and immediate.

But focusing on results is a trap. Results are influenced by luck, market conditions, and randomness. A trader can make terrible decisions and still win. A trader can make excellent decisions and still lose. Over a small sample size, results mean very little.

Process is different. Process is within your control. Process is repeatable. Process is what produces results over time.

Think about a professional athlete. They do not judge their performance by the outcome of a single game. They judge it by whether they followed their training, executed their technique, and made smart decisions under pressure. They know that if they do these things consistently, the wins will come.

Trading is the same. Your job is not to control the outcome of any single trade. Your job is to follow your process consistently and let the probabilities work in your favor over time.

This shift in focus changes how you experience trading. When you focus on results, every trade feels like a test. You are either passing or failing. The pressure is constant. The emotional swings are extreme.

When you focus on process, every trade is simply an execution. Did I follow my rules? Did I manage my risk? Did I enter and exit according to my plan? If the answer is yes, the trade was successful regardless of the outcome.

This does not mean results do not matter. They do. But results are a lagging indicator. They tell you what happened in the past. Process is a leading indicator. It predicts what will happen in the future.

The trader who focuses on process improves continuously. The trader who focuses on results is at the mercy of short-term variance.

Your trading journal should reflect this priority. Track your adherence to process, not just your P&L. Rate yourself on discipline, patience, and emotional control. These are the metrics that predict long-term success.

Results will come. But they come as a byproduct of good process, not as the primary goal.

Do you currently track your process adherence, or do you focus mainly on your profit and loss?
