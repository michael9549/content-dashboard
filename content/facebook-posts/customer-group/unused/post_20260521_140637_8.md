Jeff Olson wrote a book about something that applies perfectly to trading. He called it 'the slight edge.'

The idea is simple: small actions, repeated consistently, create massive results over time. Not because any single action is impressive, but because of the compound effect of doing it again and again.

Reading ten pages of a trading book today won't make you a better trader. Not noticeably. But reading ten pages every day for a year? That's 3,650 pages. That's a dozen books. That's a completely different trader.

Reviewing your trades today won't transform your results. But reviewing every trade for six months? Patterns emerge. Mistakes become obvious. Discipline becomes automatic.

The slight edge works both ways, though. Skipping your trading journal today won't hurt you. But skip it every day for six months? You've lost one of your most powerful tools for improvement.

Small errors compound too. Taking a slightly oversized position 'just this once.' Moving your stop 'just to give it room.' Trading when you're emotional 'just to make it back.' Each one seems harmless. But they stack. And eventually, they collapse the account.

Successful trading isn't about heroic moments. It's not about the one big trade that changes everything. It's about the slight edge. The small disciplines, repeated daily, that slowly pull you ahead of everyone else.

What small action could you take today that your future self will thank you for?
