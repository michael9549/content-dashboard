The market doesn't care about your feelings.

You can have the best strategy, the perfect setup, and all the indicators aligned. But the moment you let fear or greed take the wheel, that edge disappears.

Think about it. How many times have you entered a trade because you "had a feeling" it would work? How many times has that feeling cost you money?

Trading is not about intuition. It's about execution. The best traders aren't the ones who predict the market. They're the ones who follow their rules when it's uncomfortable.

When a trade hits your stop loss, your system is working. When you take profit at your target, your system is working. The outcome of one trade means nothing. The process means everything.

Most traders fail because they treat each trade like it matters. Like this one trade will make or break them. It won't. What breaks you is abandoning your plan because of how you feel in the moment.

Discipline is boring. It's repetitive. It's not exciting. But it's the only thing that keeps you in the game long enough to win.

The market rewards consistency, not brilliance.
