There's a moment every trader faces that determines everything.

It's not when you place your first trade. It's not when you hit your first big winner. It's not even when you blow up your first account. It's that moment after a string of losses when you're sitting there wondering if you have what it takes. When the evidence seems to suggest you should quit. When every voice in your head is saying this was a mistake.

I've faced that moment more times than I can count. Early on, I didn't handle it well. I'd quit for a week. Or I'd revenge trade and make it worse. Or I'd jump to a new strategy hoping it would save me. None of it worked. The only thing that worked was learning to sit with that discomfort. To feel it fully. And to keep going anyway.

See, that moment isn't a sign you're not cut out for trading. It's the test. Everyone who makes it to the other side has faced that exact same moment and chosen to continue. The ones who don't make it? They let that moment define them. They let it be the end of their story.

If you're in that moment right now, I want you to know something: it's normal. It's part of the journey. And it doesn't mean you're failing. It means you're being forged. Keep going. Your future self is counting on you. What would you tell yourself from six months ago?
