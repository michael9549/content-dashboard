Risk management is not a suggestion. It's the foundation everything else builds on.

You can have a 70% win rate and still blow up your account. How? By risking too much on each trade. One bad streak, and you're done.

Here's the math nobody wants to think about:

If you risk 10% per trade and lose 5 in a row, you're down 50%. Now you need to make 100% just to break even. The psychology of that hole is devastating. Most traders never climb out.

But if you risk 1% per trade and lose 5 in a row, you're down 5%. Barely a scratch. You stay calm. You stay in the game. Your edge has time to work.

The difference between 1% and 10% isn't just numbers. It's the difference between a career and a hobby. Between sleeping well at night and staring at charts at 3 AM in a panic.

Every professional trader has a hard rule: never risk more than X% on a single trade. It's not negotiable. It's not flexible. It's written in stone.

Because they know: the market doesn't care about your confidence level. It doesn't care that you're "sure" about this trade. It will humble you the moment you get cocky.

Your job isn't to maximize profits on any single trade. Your job is to survive long enough for your edge to compound.

What's your current risk per trade? Is it a fixed percentage, or does it change based on how you feel?
