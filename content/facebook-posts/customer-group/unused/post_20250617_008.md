Your edge is not your strategy. It's your execution.

Two traders can use the exact same system. Same entries, same exits, same risk management. One makes money consistently. The other loses. What's the difference?

Execution.

The winning trader follows the rules precisely. They enter when the setup appears. They exit when the plan says to exit. They risk exactly what they decided to risk. No more, no less.

The losing trader improvises. They skip setups because they "don't feel right." They hold losers hoping they'll turn around. They take profits early because they're scared of giving them back. They change position sizes based on their confidence level.

A mediocre strategy executed perfectly beats a perfect strategy executed poorly. Every time.

This is why backtesting isn't enough. You can have a system that shows 80% win rate in backtests, but if you don't execute it exactly as designed, those numbers mean nothing. Your emotions, your biases, your impulses become part of the system, and they ruin the edge.

The market doesn't reward complexity. It rewards consistency. Simple rules followed religiously will outperform complex rules followed selectively.

Your job as a trader is not to outsmart the market. It's to outsmart yourself. To follow your plan when everything in you wants to deviate.

How closely do you stick to your trading plan? Do you follow it exactly, or do you make "minor adjustments" in the moment?
