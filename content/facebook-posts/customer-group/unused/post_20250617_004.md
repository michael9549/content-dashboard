The trader who can't sit on their hands is the trader who loses money.

Patience is not passive. It's a strategy. The best opportunities don't come every hour or even every day. They come when conditions align, and that alignment is worth waiting for.

Most traders overtrade. They feel the need to be in the market constantly. If they're not in a trade, they feel like they're missing out. So they force setups that aren't there. They take lower-quality trades just to feel active.

This is expensive entertainment, not trading.

Think about fishing. The professional angler doesn't cast their line every second. They study the water. They wait for the right spot, the right time, the right conditions. Then they make one precise cast.

Trading is the same. Your capital is your line. Every trade is a cast. And every forced trade is a cast into empty water.

The market will be here tomorrow. And next week. And next year. There is no rush. The only thing that expires is your patience, and that's entirely within your control.

The traders who make money over time are the ones who can do nothing for days, then act decisively when the moment is right.

How many trades did you take this week? How many of them were truly A+ setups versus trades you took out of boredom?
