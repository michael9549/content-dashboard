There's a lie that keeps most traders stuck.

The lie is that you need to be perfect. That every trade should be a winner. That successful traders don't have losing days. I believed this lie for years. And it nearly destroyed my trading career. Because every time I had a loss, I took it personally. I made it mean something about me. About my intelligence. About my worth.

Then I met a professional trader who had been in the game for 15 years. He showed me his journal. Pages and pages of losing trades. Red days. Red weeks. Even a few red months. But here's what struck me: he treated them all the same. No emotion. No drama. Just data. 'Losses are tuition,' he said. 'You pay them to learn.'

That conversation changed everything. I realized that my problem wasn't losing. It was my relationship with losing. I was making losses mean I was a failure, when really they just meant I was a trader. Every professional loses. The difference is they don't let it derail them.

So if you're beating yourself up over a bad trade or a red day, stop. Learn what you can. Make adjustments if needed. Then move on. You're not failing. You're learning. And that's exactly what you're supposed to be doing. What would change if you stopped fearing losses?
