A professional golfer doesn't change their swing after one bad shot.

They trust the thousands of hours they put in. They know that one shot doesn't define their skill. They stay consistent because they understand the math: over time, good process produces good results.

Trading works the same way.

One losing trade doesn't mean your strategy is broken. One winning trade doesn't mean you're a genius. The only thing that matters is what happens after 100 trades, 500 trades, 1000 trades.

But here's what most traders do: they take three losses in a row and immediately start tweaking their system. They add new indicators. They change their risk per trade. They abandon what was working because of short-term noise.

This is like a golfer changing their grip after missing a putt. It makes no sense.

Your edge exists over a series of trades, not in any single trade. The moment you start reacting to individual outcomes, you've stopped trading and started gambling.

The best traders review their results monthly, not after every trade. They look for patterns in their execution, not in the market. Because they know: you can't control the market, but you can control your response to it.

How often do you review your trading performance? Daily, weekly, or only when things go wrong?
