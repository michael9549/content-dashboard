This breakdown of psychological triggers is worth understanding whether you sell or buy.

The halo effect: your first impression with a brand, person, or business influences every future interaction. That first touchpoint is so heavily weighted it colors your attitudes even when they're wrong.

This is why marketers obsess over first impressions. But here's the deeper insight: a positive first impression buffers against future negative experiences. This is where brand loyalty actually comes from. Start solid, and people give you the benefit of the doubt later.

The video mentions something important: these triggers are so powerful that even when you know they're being used, you still can't fully stop them. It's not about manipulation. It's about understanding how human decision-making actually works.

Whether you're building a business or just navigating as a consumer, knowing these biases helps. You can use them ethically to communicate better. Or you can recognize when they're being used to push you toward decisions you wouldn't otherwise make.

The goal isn't to eliminate influence. It's to be intentional about it.
