# The Real Reason Most Businesses Fail (And How to Avoid It)

Most entrepreneurs build businesses to get rich first, THEN give back later.

But here's the problem with that approach:

❌ By the time you're "ready" to give back, you may have already caused 30 years of damage
❌ You might get so addicted to profit that you never give back at all
❌ Your business becomes something you're not proud of

Anthony Tan built Grab (Southeast Asia's Uber + DoorDash + PayPal combined) differently.

From Day 1, Grab was designed as a "for-profit social enterprise."

They focused on solving REAL problems:
→ Women's safety in transportation
→ Driver income and financial inclusion
→ Economic mobility for the bottom of the pyramid

The result?

✅ They beat Uber in head-to-head competition
✅ They expanded to 8 countries
✅ They built a $40B+ company
✅ They created millions of opportunities

Here's the lesson:

When your business solves a real problem that matters to YOU...

→ You work harder than anyone else
→ You attract the best talent
→ You build something you're proud of
→ You create real impact

Your business doesn't have to choose between profit and purpose.

The most successful companies combine BOTH from the start.
