# The Barber Who Changed My Life (And Can Change Yours Too)

At 11 years old, Jay Bailey saw a black-on-black convertible Mustang GT 5.0 parked outside his barber shop.

He ran inside screaming, "Whose car is that?!"

His barber gave him the nod.

That moment changed everything.

Jay asked him, "John, I didn't know you were a dope boy."

His barber turned off the clippers and said:

"Shut up and count how many chairs you see in this shop."

"Each barber pays me $50 a week. Jay, you're smart. Do the math."

"I got two more shops just like this."

Then he said the words that rewired Jay's brain:

"I am an ENTREPRENEUR. I own this business."

"What you need to do is find something you love and make money doing it."

Two powerful things happened:

1️⃣ Jay heard the word "entrepreneur" for the first time
2️⃣ He learned the concept of OWNERSHIP

That 11-year-old kid is now the CEO of the Russell Center - the largest center in the WORLD dedicated to growing Black businesses.

Here's what I want you to understand:

→ Your environment shapes your beliefs
→ Your beliefs shape your actions
→ Your actions shape your life

Jay saw drug dealers with business skills who never had the right exposure.

He saw single mothers with more innovation than Fortune 500 CEOs.

The only difference between success and failure?

Access. Opportunity. Exposure.

Who you surround yourself with matters.

What you believe about yourself matters.

Your environment is either building you up or holding you back.

Choose wisely.

What's ONE belief about yourself you're ready to change today?
