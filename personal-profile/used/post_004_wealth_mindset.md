I learned something wild the other day.

The difference between people who make $50k a year and people who make $500k a year isn't skills. It's not even opportunities.

It's MINDSET.

I watched this woman break down the 5 mental rules that allowed her to make $100 million by age 30, and honestly? It's not complicated.

But most people will never adopt these rules. Because they're too busy being "realistic."

Here are the 5 rules that separate rich thinking from broke thinking:

**1. There's infinite money available. You're not taking from anyone.**

Most people think money is scarce. That if they make more, someone else makes less. That's poverty thinking.

Rich people understand: money flows to those who provide value. The more value you create, the more money you attract. There's no limit.

When you stop feeling guilty about wanting wealth and start seeing it as a tool to help MORE people, everything changes.

**2. Remove your fear of SPENDING money.**

Here's an exercise: Write down something you want to invest in (a course, a coach, a tool). Then write down your fear about spending that money. Then REFRAME it.

Example:
- **Want:** $2,500 marketing course
- **Fear:** "My spouse will be mad I spent this."
- **Reframe:** "My spouse will be proud when I earn $25,000 from what I learn in this course."

See the shift? Poor people focus on COST. Rich people focus on ROI.

**3. Invest in yourself first.**

The speaker tells a story about sitting front row at a conference. A woman on stage offered a $2,500 course. Most people in the room hesitated. Overthought it. Talked themselves out of it.

She bought it immediately.

Why? Because she understood that investing in herself was the fastest way to grow. And she was right.

That course led to strategies that made her millions.

Most people will spend $2,500 on a vacation they'll forget in 6 months. But won't spend it on something that could change their income forever.

**4. Wealth creation can't coexist with scarcity.**

If you're thinking small, you'll stay small. If you're thinking about "survival," you'll always be surviving.

Rich people think in terms of GROWTH and POSSIBILITY. They ask, "How can I scale this?" not "How can I scrape by?"

**5. Making more money means contributing MORE.**

When she was 19, she spent summers feeding homeless people under bridges. One-on-one. Dozens of people.

A mentor asked her: "What's the bigger impact you want to make?"

She realized: to help MILLIONS, she needed resources. And to get resources, she needed to build wealth.

Wealth isn't selfish. It's a tool to amplify your impact.

The point?

Your money problems aren't about money. They're about how you THINK about money.

Change your mindset. The money will follow.
