# Why Serving the "Bottom" Makes You Rich (The Grab Story)

Most startups want to serve wealthy customers.

Higher margins. Easier sales. Fancy offices.

But Anthony Tan built Grab differently.

He focused on serving the BOTTOM of the pyramid.

People who needed rides for 20 cents, not $200 airport trips.

Here's why this "crazy" strategy worked:

1️⃣ MASSIVE MARKET SIZE
650 million people across 8 countries.

When you serve everyone, you get scale.

2️⃣ REAL PROBLEMS = REAL LOYALTY
Solve a problem that actually hurts, and customers never leave.

3️⃣ COMPETITIVE MOAT
Uber focused on premium rides. Grab focused on the masses.

Guess who won in Southeast Asia?

4️⃣ SPEED OF EXPANSION
When your solution works for the masses, you can expand FAST.

Grab grew to 8 countries while competitors were still debating their Series A.

But here's the key insight:

They didn't sacrifice profitability.

They designed for sustainability FROM THE START.

✅ Lower cost vehicles (tuk-tuks, motorbikes)
✅ Android phones (cheaper than iPhones)
✅ Digital payments (safer for drivers)
✅ Driver financing (help them own their vehicles)

Every decision optimized for the bottom of the pyramid.

The result?

A $40B+ company that lifted millions out of poverty.

Your business doesn't need fancy customers.

It needs REAL problems to solve.

Who are YOU serving?
