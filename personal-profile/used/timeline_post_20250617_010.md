I watched a TED talk on entrepreneurial mindset, and it started with a story worth knowing.

Linda Weinman, a graphic design teacher, bought Lynda.com in 1995. She needed a sandbox to play with new design tools and a place to showcase student work. By 2002, she moved all her teaching online. LinkedIn bought the company for $1.5 billion and renamed it LinkedIn Learning.

The speaker calls this the "Yes, we can" mindset. It contradicts standard business school advice: stick to your knitting, know your core competencies, say no to things outside your expertise.

Arnold Correa, a Brazilian entrepreneur, built Atmo Digital by ignoring those rules. A client asked if he could put TVs in 260 stores across Brazil and build a satellite link for live training broadcasts. Arnold knew nothing about satellite technology. Had never worked outside Sao Paulo. But he said yes anyway. Then figured it out.

The mindset: when opportunities come, your default response determines your trajectory. "Yes, we can" doesn't mean you know how. It means you'll find out.

Most companies optimize for efficiency. Entrepreneurs optimize for opportunity. Those are different games with different rules.

When was the last time you said yes to something you didn't know how to do yet?
