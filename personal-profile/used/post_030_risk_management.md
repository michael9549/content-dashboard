I blew up my first trading account in 3 weeks.

$10,000. Gone. Not because the market was cruel. Because I was stupid.

I ignored risk management. I traded too big. I thought I could "feel" what the market would do next.

Spoiler: I couldn't.

That loss hurt. But it taught me the most valuable lesson in trading:

**Survival comes first. Profits come second.**

Every new trader wants to know: "How much can I make?"

The smart question is: "How do I make sure I'm still here in 6 months?"

Because if you blow up your account, your potential profits drop to zero.

**The three rules that saved my trading career:**

**Never risk more than 1-2% per trade.**

When I started, I'd risk 10% on a single trade. One bad streak and I was devastated. Now? A losing streak of 10 trades costs me 10-20% of my account. Annoying, but survivable.

**Stop trading your emotions.**

You just had 3 winners in a row? Great. That doesn't mean you're a genius. It means probability worked in your favor this time. Don't get cocky and increase your size.

You just had 3 losers? That doesn't mean you need to "make it back." It means you need to stick to your plan and trust the math.

**Your system is your boss.**

Not your gut. Not the news. Not some "analyst" on Twitter. Your system. If your system says take the trade, you take it. If it says stay out, you stay out.

Following a system is boring. That's the point. Boring makes money. Exciting blows up accounts.

**Here's what I wish someone told me when I started:**

Trading isn't about being right. It's about making money when you're right and losing small when you're wrong.

You can be right 40% of the time and still be wildly profitable. IF you manage your risk.

Or you can be right 70% of the time and go broke. IF you let losers run and cut winners short.

The math isn't complicated. The psychology is.

Every day, I see new traders making the same mistakes I made. Risking too much. Trading on emotion. Chasing the big win.

Some of them will learn. Most won't.

Which one are you?
