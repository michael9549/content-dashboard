# The Truth About Multitasking

Multitasking feels productive. The research says otherwise.

A Stanford study found that people who regularly multitask are actually worse at focusing, worse at filtering information, and worse at switching between tasks.

Your brain isn't built to split focus. It switches rapidly, and each switch costs you time and mental energy.

The Pomodoro Technique works because short, defined sprints prevent mental fatigue. 25 minutes of complete focus, then a 5-minute break.

One focused hour will consistently outperform three scattered ones.

Stop trying to do everything at once. Do one thing. Finish it. Move to the next.
