My feed was toxic.

Not in the obvious way. No drama. No negativity. Just... comparison.

Everyone was winning. Everyone was scaling. Everyone was crushing it.

Except me. Or so it felt.

---

**The subtle poison:**

I'd open Instagram and immediately feel behind.

"They're at $100K months. I'm not."
"They have a team. I don't."
"They make it look easy. Why is this so hard for me?"

I wasn't inspired. I was discouraged.

---

**So I did something drastic.**

I unfollowed almost everyone.

500 people. Gone.

I kept a handful of accounts that genuinely educated or inspired me. Everyone else? Unfollowed.

---

**The result:**

My anxiety dropped. My focus improved. I stopped comparing my chapter 3 to their chapter 20.

I started running my own race again.

---

**Curate your inputs like your business depends on it.**

Because it does.

What you consume shapes what you believe. What you believe shapes what you do. What you do shapes your results.

Garbage in, garbage out. Inspiration in, action out.

---

**Your feed should energize you, not deflate you.**

If it doesn't, you have permission to unfollow. Even if it's "good" content. Even if you "should" find it motivating.

Your mental real estate is valuable. Protect it.
