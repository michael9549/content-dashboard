I used to think success was about skills.

Learn the right strategies. Master the right tools. Work harder than everyone else.

I was wrong.

Skills matter, but they're not the differentiator. Not anymore. Everyone has access to the same information, the same courses, the same tools.

What separates people who succeed from people who don't?

**The people they know.**

Not in a sleazy "networking event" way. In a real, human, "who would vouch for you" way.

Here's what I've learned after years in this game:

**One introduction beats 100 cold emails.**

You can spend months trying to get a meeting with the right person. Or you can know someone who makes one text. The second path is instant. The first path is exhausting.

**Your network determines your opportunities.**

The best deals never hit the public market. The best jobs never get posted. The best partnerships happen over coffee between people who already trust each other.

If you're not in those rooms, you don't even know what you're missing.

**Three ways to build a network that actually matters:**

1. **Add value first.** Don't ask for anything. Find out what someone needs and help them get it. Do this enough times and people start bringing opportunities to YOU.

2. **Show up consistently.** One conference won't change your life. But showing up every month, being helpful, being visible? That compounds faster than any investment.

3. **Connect other people.** The most powerful person in any room isn't the one with the most money. It's the one who knows everyone and makes introductions. Be that person.

Your skills get you in the door. Your network determines which doors exist.

Stop trying to do everything alone. Start building relationships that multiply your efforts.

The ROI on a strong network is infinite.
