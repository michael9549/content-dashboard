I lost $10,000 on a single decision.

Not in trading. In business.

I hired the wrong agency. I ignored red flags. I trusted the slick presentation over my gut.

Three months later, I had nothing to show for it except a lighter bank account and a bruised ego.

---

**But here's the thing:**

That $10,000 loss taught me more than any course ever could.

It taught me to trust my instincts. To do proper due diligence. To never outsource what I didn't understand.

It was expensive tuition. But it was worth every penny.

---

**The mistake wasn't hiring help.**

The mistake was abdicating responsibility. I wanted someone else to fix my problems. I wanted a magic solution.

There isn't one. Never has been. Never will be.

---

**What I do differently now:**

I learn before I delegate
I start small and test
I trust patterns over promises
I take full responsibility for outcomes

---

**Your expensive mistakes aren't failures.**

They're the price of real education. The kind you can't get from a book.

The key is extracting the lesson. Making sure the cost wasn't wasted.

---

**I've made plenty of mistakes since then.**

But I've never made that same one again. And that has saved me far more than $10,000.
