# The 20-Hour Rule

Forget the 10,000-hour myth.

You don't need 10,000 hours to learn something new. You need 20.

Research shows that with 20 hours of focused, deliberate practice, you can go from knowing nothing to being reasonably good at almost any skill.

That's 45 minutes a day for about a month.

The key is to:
1. Deconstruct the skill (break it down)
2. Learn enough to self-correct
3. Remove distractions
4. Practice for at least 20 hours

The biggest barrier isn't intellectual. It's emotional. We don't like feeling stupid at the beginning.

Push through the first 20 hours. That's where the magic happens.
