I started my first business with a lot of wrong assumptions.

I thought success would be fast. I thought I could figure it out as I went. I thought hard work guaranteed results.

I was wrong about all of it.

---

**Here's what I wish someone had told me:**

**Your first business probably won't be your last.**

And that's okay. The first one is where you learn. Expect it to be messy. Expect to make mistakes. You're paying tuition.

**Revenue isn't profit.**

I celebrated $10K months while ignoring that I was spending $9K to make them. Don't be me. Watch your margins from day one.

**The people who buy first are rarely the people you expected.**

I built for one audience. A completely different audience bought. Stay open. The market tells you who you serve.

**Consistency beats intensity.**

I would work 12-hour days for a week, then burn out and do nothing for two. Slow and steady actually does win this race.

**You can't do it alone.**

I tried. I failed faster. Find mentors, communities, accountability partners. Your ego will resist. Do it anyway.

---

**Most importantly:**

The struggle is the path. There is no version where it's easy at the start. The difficulty is what filters out the people who don't really want it.

Keep going.

---

**If you're in your first year:**

You're doing better than you think. Keep learning. Keep adjusting. Keep showing up.

The breakthrough is closer than it feels.
