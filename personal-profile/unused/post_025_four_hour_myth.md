Everyone wants the 4-hour workweek.

Nobody wants to hear what it actually takes to get there.

I see the posts. "I work 2 hours a day and make $50k/month."

What they don't show you?

The 5 years of 80-hour weeks to build the systems.

The failed businesses that almost bankrupted them.

The relationships that suffered while they were obsessed.

The truth about passive income is that it's never actually passive. It's just front-loaded.

You do the work NOW so you don't have to do it later. But you still have to do the work.

**Here's the real timeline nobody talks about:**

**Years 1-2:** You're grinding. Learning. Failing. Probably making less than minimum wage if you divide your income by your hours. This is where 90% of people quit.

**Years 3-4:** Things start clicking. You've got some momentum. Maybe you hire your first person. You're still working hard, but now there's light at the end of the tunnel.

**Years 5+:** Now you have options. Systems are running. Teams are handling the day-to-day. You can step back if you want. Or you can keep building. The choice is yours.

The 4-hour workweek isn't a starting point. It's a destination.

And you don't get there by working 4 hours.

You get there by working 80 hours a week on the RIGHT things until you don't have to anymore.

So if you're in year one and you're exhausted, good. That means you're doing it right.

Keep building. The payoff is coming.

But it's not coming tomorrow. And it's definitely not coming in 4 hours.
