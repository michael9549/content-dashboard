I asked 100 people if they wanted to start a business.

87 said yes.

I checked back a year later.

3 had actually started.

What happened to the other 84? Same thing that always happens.

They were waiting.

Waiting for the right time. Waiting to save more money. Waiting to learn more. Waiting to feel ready.

They're still waiting.

**The paradox of starting:**

You think you need confidence to start. But confidence only comes from doing.

You think you need knowledge to begin. But knowledge only comes from experience.

You think you need resources to launch. But resources only come from momentum.

Everything you think you need before starting? You actually get it BY starting.

**The three lies people tell themselves:**

**"I don't have enough money."**

I started my first business with $200. Most service businesses require almost nothing to start. You don't need funding. You need a customer.

**"I don't have enough time."**

You have the same 24 hours as everyone else. The difference is how you use them. Netflix or side hustle? Sleep in or wake up early? Scrolling or creating?

Time isn't the issue. Priorities are.

**"I need to learn more first."**

This is the most dangerous lie. Because learning feels productive. It feels like progress.

But learning without application is just entertainment. You don't need another course. You need to ship something.

**What the 3 people who started did differently:**

They started before they were ready.

They launched imperfect products. They made embarrassing mistakes. They looked foolish sometimes.

But they learned. They iterated. They improved.

While everyone else was preparing, they were practicing. And practice beats preparation every time.

**The real reason people don't start:**

Fear.

Fear of failure. Fear of judgment. Fear of finding out they're not as capable as they hope.

Starting a business means risking your ego. It means discovering your limits. It means facing the possibility that you might not be good enough.

That's terrifying.

But here's the thing: Not starting is also a choice. And it's a choice that guarantees the outcome you're afraid of.

**The question:**

A year from now, do you want to be someone who started? Or someone who's still waiting to feel ready?

The clock is ticking.
