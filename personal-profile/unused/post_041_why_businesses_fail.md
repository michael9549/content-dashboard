It's not the economy.

It's not the algorithm. It's not the competition. It's not bad luck.

The real reason most online businesses fail?

They quit too soon.

---

**Here's the pattern I see over and over:**

Month 1-3: Excitement. Everything feels possible.
Month 4-6: Reality hits. Results are slower than expected.
Month 7-9: Doubt creeps in. "Maybe this isn't for me."
Month 10-12: They quit. Right before things would have clicked.

---

**The 18-month rule:**

I've noticed something after watching hundreds of entrepreneurs.

The ones who make it almost always have one thing in common: they gave it at least 18 months before judging their results.

Not 3 months. Not 6. 18.

That's how long it takes to:

Find your voice
Build real skills
Earn trust
Figure out what actually works

---

**Most people quit in the gap.**

The gap between starting and succeeding. The messy middle where nothing seems to work.

But that gap is where everyone who made it had to walk too.

---

**If you're in month 6 and struggling:**

Good. That means you're normal. That means you're learning. That means you're closer than the person who quit at month 5.

Keep going.

The breakthrough usually comes right after the moment you want to quit most.
