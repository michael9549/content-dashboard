I used to make decisions based on how I felt.

Excited? Say yes. Nervous? Say no. Comfortable? Do it. Uncomfortable? Avoid it.

My feelings were terrible decision-makers.

---

**Then I built a framework.**

Now every major decision goes through the same filter. No exceptions.

---

**The 5-Question Framework:**

1. What's the best-case scenario if this works?
2. What's the worst-case scenario if this fails?
3. Can I survive the worst case?
4. What's the cost of doing nothing?
5. Will I regret not trying this in 5 years?

---

**Question 1 and 2 force me to define the range.**

No more vague hopes or undefined fears. Concrete upside. Concrete downside.

**Question 3 keeps me from ruin.**

If the worst case ends me, it's probably not worth it. No matter how good the upside looks.

**Question 4 reveals hidden costs.**

Sometimes inaction is the riskiest move of all.

**Question 5 cuts through short-term noise.**

Fear fades. Regret compounds.

---

**This framework has saved me from bad deals.**

It's also pushed me into opportunities my feelings would have talked me out of.

---

**Your feelings are data, not instructions.**

Listen to them. Then run them through a framework that actually serves your long-term interests.
