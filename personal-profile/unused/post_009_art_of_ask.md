# The Proposal That Failed Spectacularly (And What It Teaches Us About Marketing)

Curtis planned the perfect proposal.

Music festival. Big stage. Crowd watching.

He got down on one knee and said:

"Jackie, baby girl... you're like an angel in my life."

"The writing is on the wall. I want to spend the rest of my life with you."

"Jackie, will you marry me?"

Jackie looked him dead in the eyes and said:

"I'm sorry, but no."

Then walked off the stage.

😬

Here's why this matters for YOUR business:

Every day, you're making proposals.

→ Buy my product
→ Join my program
→ Sign up for my service

And just like Curtis, you might be doing it at the WRONG time.

The 3 Keys to Making the Perfect Ask:

1️⃣ TIMING IS EVERYTHING
Don't propose on the first date.

Stories slow down the process and build trust FIRST.

Give value before you ask.

2️⃣ BUILD CONNECTION
Stories inject the human element into your business.

People buy from people they connect with.

3️⃣ MAKE IT THE LOGICAL NEXT STEP
When you tell the right story, your offer becomes obvious.

"This is exactly what I need."

The best marketers aren't pushy.

They're storytellers who make the ask feel inevitable.

What's your "proposal" strategy?

Are you building connection first?

Or going straight for the ask?
