I know traders who can predict market direction with 70% accuracy and still lose money.

I also know traders who are right less than half the time and consistently pull six figures from the markets every year.

The difference isn't strategy. It's risk management.

**The Math That Destroys Most Traders**

Let me show you something that should change how you think about trading forever.

Scenario A: You risk 10% of your account per trade. You have a 60% win rate. Your average winner is 1.5x your average loser.

Sounds decent, right? Good win rate, positive risk-reward.

After just 10 trades, you have a 35% chance of blowing up your account. After 50 trades, it's nearly inevitable.

Scenario B: You risk 1% of your account per trade. Same 60% win rate. Same 1.5:1 reward-to-risk.

Now you have virtually zero chance of ruin. And with the same edge, you'll compound steady gains over time.

The only difference? Position sizing.

**Why We Ignore Risk Management**

If risk management is so obviously important, why do most traders neglect it?

Because it's not sexy. Learning a new strategy feels like gaining an edge. Managing risk feels like limiting your potential. When you're confident in a setup, risking 1% feels ridiculous. You "know" this trade is going to work.

Until it doesn't.

And here's the thing about probabilities: They don't care about your confidence. A 70% win rate still loses 30% of the time. If you're risking too much, those losses will eventually destroy you.

**The Risk Management Framework That Saves Accounts**

After blowing up two accounts early in my trading career, I developed a simple framework that I never deviate from:

**The 1% Rule**: Never risk more than 1% of your account on a single trade. Ever. No exceptions. Not when you're "sure." Not when you need to "make it back." 1% maximum.

**The 6% Rule**: If you lose 6% of your account in a single day, you're done trading for the day. Not because you're emotional (though you probably are), but because something is wrong. Your reads are off. The market conditions have changed. Step away and reassess.

**The 10% Rule**: If you lose 10% of your account in a single week, you're done for the week. This seems extreme until you realize that preserving capital is more important than capturing every opportunity. There will always be more trades. There won't always be more capital.

**Position Sizing Made Simple**

Here's a practical formula I use for every trade:

1. Determine your stop loss (in pips/points)
2. Calculate position size so that if stop is hit, you lose exactly 1%
3. If the position size is too small to be worth trading, skip the trade

Most traders do this backwards. They decide how much they want to make, then figure out position size. That's how you end up risking 5% or 10% without realizing it.

**The Psychology of Preservation**

Risk management isn't just math. It's psychology.

When you know that no single trade can hurt you significantly, you trade differently. You don't hesitate on entries. You don't move stops out of fear. You don't revenge trade after a loss.

You can be objective because you're not emotionally invested in any individual outcome.

This is the state you need to be in to actually execute your strategy properly. And you can't get there without proper risk management.

**The Compounding Effect**

Here's what most traders miss: Risk management doesn't just prevent blow-ups. It enables compounding.

A trader risking 5% per trade might make 50% in a good month. But they'll also have drawdowns that wipe out months of gains. Their equity curve looks like a roller coaster.

A trader risking 1% per trade might only make 10% in a good month. But their drawdowns are small and recoverable. Their equity curve trends steadily upward.

Over a year, the conservative trader often ends up with more money—not because they had better trades, but because they stayed in the game long enough for their edge to play out.

**The Only Edge That Matters**

You can have the perfect strategy, the best psychology, and years of experience. But if you don't manage risk, none of it matters.

Risk management is the foundation that everything else builds on. It's not exciting. It's not what gets views on YouTube. But it's what separates professionals from gamblers.

And if you want to trade for a living, you need to be a professional.
