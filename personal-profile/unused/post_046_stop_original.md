I used to stress about being original.

Every piece of content had to be groundbreaking. Never seen before. Totally unique.

The result? I barely created anything. The bar was too high.

---

**Then I learned a secret:**

Nothing is original.

Every idea is a remix. Every insight is built on someone else's insight. Every creator is standing on shoulders.

The people you admire? They borrowed heavily from the people they admired.

---

**What actually matters:**

Your unique perspective. Your specific experiences. Your voice.

The topic might be done a thousand times. But YOU haven't done it.

Your story. Your lens. Your way of explaining.

That's the value. Not the idea itself.

---

**I stopped asking:** "Has this been said before?"

**I started asking:** "Has this been said by ME before?"

If the answer is no, I write it.

---

**Your audience doesn't need novelty.**

They need clarity. They need to hear the same truth in a way that finally clicks for them.

Sometimes the 100th explanation is the one that changes everything.

---

**Create without the burden of originality.**

Say what you believe. Share what you know. Tell your story.

The world doesn't need more unique ideas. It needs more honest voices.

Be one of them.
