I thought I was busy.

I thought I was working hard. I thought I was being productive.

Then I tracked every single hour for 30 days.

And I was horrified.

---

**The truth about my "busy" schedule:**

2+ hours per day on social media (not posting, just scrolling)
1.5 hours in "prep mode" (planning to work, not actually working)
45 minutes recovering from distractions (getting back into flow)
Countless hours on tasks that didn't move the needle

I wasn't busy. I was distracted.

---

**The 80/20 became obvious:**

20% of my time created 80% of my results.

The other 80%? Motion disguised as action. Comfort zone activities. Things that felt productive but weren't.

---

**What I changed:**

Time-blocking my deep work
Eliminating scroll time before noon
Batching low-value tasks
Saying no to anything that didn't serve my main goal

---

**The result:**

I accomplished more in 2 weeks than I used to in 2 months.

Not because I worked more hours. Because I worked the right hours on the right things.

---

**You don't need more time.**

You need honest awareness of how you're spending the time you have.

Track it. I dare you. You might not like what you see, but you'll never unsee it.
