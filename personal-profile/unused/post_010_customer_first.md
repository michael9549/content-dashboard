# The $1.5 Billion Lesson From a Graphic Design Teacher

In 1995, Lynda Weinman bought a domain name.

She just wanted a "sandbox" to play with new design software.

Photoshop. Illustrator. The new digital tools.

She also needed a place to put her students' work.

Fast forward to 2015:

LinkedIn bought Lynda.com for $1.5 BILLION.

Here's what most people miss about this story:

Lynda didn't start with a business plan.

She started with a PROBLEM she personally had.

→ She needed to learn new software
→ She needed to teach her students
→ She needed a place to showcase work

She built the solution for HERSELF first.

Then she realized:

"Wait... other people have this problem too."

The lesson?

The best businesses come from solving problems you ACTUALLY have.

Not problems you think other people have.

Not problems you researched in a market analysis.

Problems you LIVE with every day.

When you solve your own problem:

✅ You understand it deeply
✅ You care about the solution
✅ You know when it's actually working
✅ You attract others like you

Lynda's "unconventional" path:

→ Started as a teacher
→ Built tools for her students
→ Expanded to online courses
→ Sold for $1.5B

Your "unconventional" path might be your competitive advantage.

What problem are YOU solving that you personally experience?
